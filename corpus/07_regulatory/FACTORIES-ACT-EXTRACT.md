# Factories Act, 1948 — Sections relevant to process safety management

**Source:** https://www.indiacode.nic.in/handle/123456789/1530

Clause text below is assembled from publicly available search-engine-indexed
excerpts of this standard, not a full verbatim transcription of the official
PDF. Verify exact clause/sub-section numbers against the source above before
relying on this for a compliance audit.

### FA-1948-S7A (p.1)

Every occupier shall ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health, safety and welfare of all workers while they are at work in the factory, and shall prepare and revise a written statement of general policy with respect to health and safety.

### FA-1948-S41C (p.1)

The occupier of a factory involving a hazardous process shall maintain accurate health records of workers exposed to it, appoint qualified persons to supervise the handling of hazardous substances, and arrange medical examinations before, during, and after exposure.

### FA-1948-S87 (p.1)

The State Government may, by rules, declare a manufacturing process carried on in any specified class of factories to be a dangerous operation, and may make rules imposing further safety and health requirements on it.
